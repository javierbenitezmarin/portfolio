# Communication

I treat communication as part of the engineering, not an afterthought. Most of the trouble I
have seen on projects came from alignment gaps, not from the code.

## How I approach it

- **Match the room.** An exec, a PM, and an engineer need the same truth told three different
  ways. I change the framing, not the facts.
- **Lead with the point.** I say the decision or the ask first, then back it up. People are busy.
- **No surprises.** I would rather flag a risk early and be wrong than stay quiet and be right
  too late.
- **Prepare the presentation.** How a project is shown matters, especially to non-technical
  stakeholders. I put real effort into making the story and the visuals land.

## What I believe

- Alignment beats heroics. A team on the same page moves faster than a lone expert.
- If the listener did not get it, that is on me, not them.
- Keeping people in the loop is cheap. Fixing a misalignment later is not.
